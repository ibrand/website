Chest

1.
Suddenly, there's space in here.
When I breathe in, the whole atrium fills and everywhere the air touches gently expands to receive it just like the edges of the universe.
I've never breathed like this before.

2.
It becomes clear why I feel so fixated on the atrium in the first place,
The conceptual capacious room filled with hard surfaces that echo when you speak inside it
Was a roundabout way of imagining the void of the universe as finite, as reflective, as contained

3.

Ironically hearing myself attempting to "breathe deeply" on this track I hear how strained that concept was.

Mom tells me that I need to breathe from my diaphragm not from the shallow body of my lungs.

I am listening to the guitar tick like clockwork as I practice breathing. At that time, breathing in a trance. Living just a few inches outside the confines of my body in a overpowering place of dysphoric euphoria in a place of revelation, a direct conduit from the divine through me.

4.
"What would it feel like to peer into that cup?"
He asks me as I explain that my cup used to run over and now the water sits inside it like the lakes that pulled me over the finish line.

"Back then, the divine used to flow straight through me, because I wasn't present enough to slow it down" I say, "these days, it pools up inside me and I have so much space to hold it in"

What does it mean to contain the universe?
